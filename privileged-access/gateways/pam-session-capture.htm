Session recording

Session recording allows teams to securely record a complete and accurate history of individual Secure Shell (SSH) and Remote Desktop (RDP) sessions. Teams can use these recordings for audit, training, or server monitoring purposes.

Storage

During a session, the gateway temporarily stores files at a specified location. After a session ends, teams can store the finalized session logs locally on the gateway or upload them to remote platforms such as (AWS) S3 or Google Cloud Storage (GCS). See Configure the [%=okta-feature-names.Okta Privileged Access%] gateway.

Session logs are formatted to include a UTC timestamp, the team name, and the account of the user. For example, a sample session log filename is YYYYMMDDTHHMMSS.SSSS-teamName-userName.asa.

Signing and Encryption

signs session logs to provide integrity. This prevents attackers from manipulating a log file to hide their actions. New signing keys are generated roughly every 24 hours.
