Set up Desktop MFA for Windows

Desktop MFA for Windows protects your computer and data by ensuring that only you can sign in to your Windows computer. is used to configure offline authentication methods in addition to the MFA methods that you might already be familiar with (for example, a push notification or a one-time password). After signing in to your Windows computer, you're prompted to add at least one offline sign-in method to confirm your identity. Having an offline sign-in method allows you to gain access to your computer, even without an internet connection.

In the Okta Verify app, the Accounts tab is used to authenticate your identity when you have an internet connection. To gain access to your computer when you aren't online, you're prompted to add one or more offline authentication methods. These offline methods appear on the Device Access tab in the Okta Verify app.

Start this task

  1. Start your Windows computer.

  2. Enter your username and password.

  3. If you don't have any offline authentication methods set up, enroll at least one method to sign in to Windows when your computer is offline. Choose one of the following methods.

Create an offline one-time password

  1. Click One-time password.

  2. On your mobile device, install the app. If the app is already installed, open the app.

  3. In the app, tap Add accountOrganizationYes, ready to scan.

  4. Scan the QR code shown on your computer display.

  5. On your computer, click Next in the offline one-time password setup wizard. A new account appears in your Okta Verify app on your mobile device.

  6. In the app on your mobile device, view the number in the account you just added.

  7. Enter this number in the setup wizard on your computer.

  8. Click Next.

You can now sign in to Windows with your offline one-time password.

Enable for offline authentication

  1. Insert your YubiKey into a USB slot on your computer.

  2. Click .

    If you receive an error saying YubiKey was not detected, remove the YubiKey, and then reinsert it. Click Try again or Back to select a different offline authentication method.

You can now use Offline to sign in to Windows when your computer is offline.
