Okta Verify includes several enhanced features, including the following:

Okta Verify with Push Authentication

Push notifications enable users to verify their identity with a single tap on their mobile device—without the need to type a code. This gets them directly into their apps while retaining the same level of security. This feature is available for iPhone, Android and Windows devices.

For more information about using Okta Verify Multifactor Authentication (MFA), see Configuring Multifactor Authentication.

To enable this feature, do the following:

  1. Log into Okta as an administrator.
  2. Navigate to Security > Multifactor page.
  3. Click the Factor Type tab.
  4. Click the Edit button.
  5. Under Acceptable factors, check the Okta Verify > Enable Push Notification, as shown below.

  1. Click the Save button.

End User Experience After Enablement

End users experience the following on their next sign in to Okta.

After successfully signing into Okta, the Sign in with Okta Verify challenge screen appears.

Ensure you have access to your mobile device as you are prompted to verify by either:

  • Selecting Push to device to use push authentication. OR
  • Clicking the Okta Mobile app from your mobile device and entering the one-time code into the Enter code field. If you opted to configure Okta Verify manually, you will only have the option to enter a code, not to push to your device.

If you opted for push authentication, you have two options:

Have a notification sent out to your device

Tap the Approve icon on your device to approve the verification request.

You are signed into your account without any further action.

Use the automatic push option

Click the Send push automatically check-box.

For this first instance, you are still required to click the Push to device button on the Okta Sign On page. After you've completed this initial verification, simply touching the Approve button from your mobile device signs you into Okta without further action.

Upgrading to Okta Verify with Push

If you want to prompt your users to upgrade to a version of Okta Verify that supports Push Authentication, first enable that functionality. For details see Admin Configuration, above. When enabled, the next time your end users use Okta Verify, they will see a Please update your profile screen with a button allowing them to immediately upgrade. Your end users can either upgrade or click Remind me later to continue without upgrading. If they chose to be reminded, a prompt will be shown the next time they sign in.

Okta Verify with Touch ID

Touch ID uses Touch ID technology to guard against unauthorized use of Okta Verify. Admins can configure an end-user fingerprint request, which appears after the initial MFA challenge. For details, see Okta Verify with Touch ID.

Admin Configuration

To enable Okta Verify, do the following:

  1. Log into Okta as an administrator.

  2. Navigate to Security > Multifactor page.

  3. Click the Factor Type tab.
  4. Click the Edit button.
  5. Under Acceptable factors, check Okta Verify. See above for information on enabling Push Notification and Touch ID.
  6. Click the Save button.

The previous steps allow for an org-level update. If you wish to enable Okta Verify with Push on a per-group level, see Using the Multifactor Page.

End User Configuration

The first time end users sign into their orgs after you configure this factor, they see the Extra verification is required for your account page. To being the setup process, they must do the following:

  1. Click the Setup button.
  2. From your mobile device, follow the instructions to download and install the Okta Verify app, then click Next.
  3. Configure Okta Verify to link it to your Okta account. You can scan a QR code or manually enter a code.

Setup using a QR Code

  1. On your phone, start the Okta Verify app, tap the Add Account button.
  2. Scan the QR code on your computer screen using your device camera.

The pass code generator screen appears and generates pass codes to use when prompted for extra verification. You have 30 seconds to enter the pass code before it generates a new one.

Manual Configuration

You can also configure an account manually by doing the following:

  1. On your mobile device, start the Okta Verify app, then tap the Add Account button.
  2. From the barcode page, tap the No barcode? button.
  3. In the Okta Account field, enter your Okta username (for example, ted@mycompany.com).
  4. From your computer, click the Can’t scan the QR code link so that you can get the secret key and enter it in the Secret Key field on your mobile device.
  5. Tap the Save button.

The pass code generator screen appears and generates the codes to use when prompted for extra verification. You have 30 seconds to enter the pass code before it generates a new one.

Using Multiple Accounts

End users can use Okta Verify for multiple accounts, and even organizations outside of Okta.

Adding an Account

To add a new account, users simply tap the Add Account button. When multiple accounts exist, they are displayed in the order through which they were enrolled—the oldest on top.

Reordering Accounts

To reorder existing accounts, tap the Edit button to access edit mode. Simply hold the gripper icon on the right-side of the tile and drag to the preferred location.

Editing an Account Name

To edit an account name, first tap the Edit button to the top-right corner to enter edit mode, then tap the organization name you wish to change.

Deleting an Account

To delete an existing account, tap the Edit button to access the edit view. Tapping the red delete icon removes the account from the list. Before the delete action is completed, end users are served with a warning modal to verify the choice. Deletion of an account is permanent and prevents sign on for the account.

Apple Watch and Android Wear

Okta Verify is also available as an Apple Watch or Android Wear app. Your end users can search for and install Okta Verify as they would any other app. If Okta Verify is configured as an authentication factor for your org, your end users can easily link Okta Verify to their Okta account, as described in Configuring Multifactor Authentication.

Apple Watch

The Okta Verify for Apple Watch app allows you to view and accept or deny Okta Verify with Push challenges from your watch screen. The watch also displays a rotating one-time password to allow authentication if Push is not enabled, or if internet connectivity is unavailable.

Once you've paired your watch with an iPhone, the Okta Verify app installs automatically. Note that while the app can be opened and used at any time, the watch will only receive notifications from the iPhone if it is locked. This is per Apple design.

Android Wear

The Okta Verify for Android Wear app allows you to view and accept or deny Okta Verify with Push challenges from your watch screen. End users can also access the OTP on their Android Wear devices.
