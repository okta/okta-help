Upgrade from Factor Sequencing to Assurance Models

In Okta Classic Engine, Factor Sequencing allows end users to sign in to their org by authenticating with a series of configured multifactor authentication (MFA) factors in place of a standard password. In Okta Identity Engine, we’ve enhanced this concept and replaced Factor Sequencing with new assurance-based sign-on policy settings that offer greater flexibility when configuring app-level policies for each app in your org. Now, you can configure passwordless authentication for one app, and MFA for another instead of applying a one-size-fits-all solution.

To help you prepare to use our new assurance-based sign-on policy settings, we’ll take you through two scenarios and show you how to make the transition.

Scenario 1: Factor Sequencing is enabled in Okta Classic Engine, but you have no active chains

First, determine whether Factor Sequencing is enabled in your org or not:

  1. In Okta Classic Engine, go to the Admin Console and click Security > Authentication. The Authentication page appears.

  2. Click the Sign On tab.

  3. Select one of the Okta Sign-on Policies in the list.

  4. Click the pencil icon for one of the rules in the list. The Edit Rule dialog appears.

  5. In the Authentication section, verify whether Password / Any IdP or Password / Any IdP + Any factor is selected.

  1. If either of these options is selected, Factor Sequencing chains are not active in all the rules in your org’s App Sign-on Policies, and you can follow the instructions below for this scenario.

Although you don’t have any Factor Sequencing chains Okta Classic Engine, when activated, the Factor Sequencing feature provides an identifier-first experience to the end users of your Okta Classic Engine tenant. An identifier-first experience is when the Sign-In Widget only shows the Username field, and not the Password field, as in this example:

To upgrade to Okta Identity Engine and preserve the identifier-first flow, do the following:

Note:

  • If the Password / Any IdP + Any factor setting is selected in any of your Okta Sign-On Policy rules in Okta Identity Engine, skip steps 3 to 5 of this procedure.

  • If you don’t need to use the identifier-first experience in Okta Identity Engine, skip steps 3 to 6 of this procedure.

  1. Disable the Factor Sequencing Feature Flag by contacting Okta Support.

  2. Upgrade to Okta Identity Engine and sign in.

  3. On the Okta Admin Console, click Directories > Groups, create an empty group, and name it “Test”.

  1. On the Okta Admin Console, click Security > Okta Sign-on Policy. The Okta Sign-on Policy page appears.

  2. Create a new Okta Sign-on Policy and assign it to the “Test” group you just created.

  1. On the Okta Sign-on Policy page, select the policy you just created and click Add Rule. The Add Rule dialog appears.

  2. In the And Primary factor is section, select the Password / IDP / any factor allowed by app sign on rules option.

  3. Click Create Rule.

Scenario 2: Factor Sequencing is enabled in Okta Classic Engine, and you have active chains

First, determine whether you have Factor Sequencing chains enabled in your org or not:

  1. In Okta Classic Engine, go to the Admin Console and click Security > Authentication. The Authentication page appears.

  2. Click the Sign On tab.

  3. Select one of the Okta Sign-on Policies in the list.

  4. Click the pencil icon for one of the rules in the list. The Edit Rule dialog appears.

  5. In the Authentication section, verify whether Factor Sequence is selected in any of the rules.

  6. If Factor Sequence isn’t selected, go to Scenario 1 and follow the instructions there. Otherwise, if it is selected, you’ll see a Factor Sequence Chain, such as this one:

  1. Continue with the procedure below.

Upgrade your Factor Sequences from Okta Classic Engine to Assurance Models in Okta Identity Engine

We recommend that you test this procedure in a preview environment that mirrors your production environment before deploying the changes to production.

  1. Evaluate whether Okta’s new assurance-based model meets your needs.

  2. Design new app sign-on policies in Okta Identity Engine that provide security outcomes and protection similar to what your Factor Sequencing rules in Okta Classic Engine provide.

  3. Determine whether you can turn off your current Okta Classic Engine Factor Sequencing rules in production environments for the time you need to perform the upgrade.

  4. Delete all Okta sign-on policy rules that use Factor Sequencing, or, if you want to keep them for reference, edit them so they use the Password / Any IDP or Password / IDP + Any factor option instead of the Factor Sequence option.

  5. Contact Okta Support and ask them to disable the Factor Sequencing feature flag.

  6. Upgrade to Okta Identity Engine.

  7. Configure and activate your new Okta Identity Engine app sign-on policies for all your apps.

  8. Test your scenarios and ensure that everything works as expected.

  9. Delete all old Factor Sequencing rules from Okta Classic Engine.
