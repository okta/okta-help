Migrate Device Trust to Okta FastPass

Okta Identity Engine is gradually rolling out to customers. For upgrade eligibility, contact your account representative.

Use this procedure to upgrade Device Trust (Classic Engine) to Device Trust (Okta Identity Engine) and Okta FastPass on the Okta Identity Engine. During this procedure you will:

  1. Identify the types of devices within your organization: desktop (Windows or macOS), mobile, or desktop (Windows or macOS) and mobile.
  2. If you have any mobile devices using Device Trust (Classic Engine), turn off Mobile Device Trust.
  3. Upgrade from an Okta Classic Engine to an Okta Identity Engine.
  4. Verify that the Device Trust policies from your Okta Classic Engine were transferred to the Okta Identity Engine.
  5. Enable Okta FastPass for all users.
  6. Remove Device Trust (Classic Engine) and decommission your IWA servers.

IWA and Device Trust (Classic Engine) are not supported on the Identity Engine. This procedure allows you to maintain Device Trust (Classic Engine) functionality until you have Okta FastPass rolled out and are using Okta Verify in your organization. It is not intended to be Device Trust (Classic Engine) support for Identity Engine. In the future, you will be required to use Okta FastPass and Okta Verify to ensure continuity of Device Trust (Classic Engine) on Identity Engine. You will not disable Device Trust in your Identity Engine until the upgrade is complete (all users are using Okta FastPass).

Modifications to Device Trust (Classic Engine) are not available after you upgrade your org. Once you upgrade to Identity Engine, Device Trust (Classic Engine) functionality will continue to work as it was configured; however, you will no longer have administrative capability to modify or change configurations.

See Compare Identity Engine and Classic Engine, Deploying Device Trust with the Okta Identity Engine, and Limitations to learn more about the differences between the Classic Engine and the Identity Engine.

Before you begin

Carefully read through the following information before you begin the migration from Device Trust to Okta FastPass:

  • If your Classic Engine has Integrated Windows Authentication (IWA) Agents configured, take a record of your configuration settings. IWA configuration settings are no longer available in the Okta Admin Console after you upgrade to the Identity Engine. To help preserve rollback scenarios, it is also strongly recommended that changes to IWA not be made after you upgrade to Identity Engine. In the Okta Admin Console, go to Security > Delegated Authentication. In the IWA Agents section, take a note of your configuration settings.

  • Using your mobile device management (MDM) tool, take an inventory of all devices that have the Device Trust (Classic Engine) certificates. This helps to ensure that the same devices continue to work on Identity Engine, after you upgrade your org to Identity Engine.

  • End users require the latest version of Okta Verify. Okta Verify registers the device in the Universal Directory. Okta Verify detects the presence of management certs on the device, to attest that a device is managed or trusted. The latest version is required for Okta FastPass.

Start this procedure

  • Task 1: Identify the types of devices that you have within your organization: desktop (Windows or macOS), mobile, or desktop (Windows or macOS) and mobile
  • Task 2: In your Classic Engine org, prepare mobile devices for the migration
  • Task 3: Upgrade from Classic Engine to Identity Engine
  • Task 4: Verify that Device Trust works on Okta Identity Engine
  • Task 5: Enable Okta FastPass for some users and verify that it works as expected
  • Task 6: Enable Okta FastPass for all users
  • Task 7: Remove Device Trust and decommission your IWA servers

Task 1: Identify the types of devices that you have within your organization: desktop (Windows or macOS), mobile, or desktop (Windows or macOS) and mobile

During the migration, only desktop (Windows or macOS) devices are transferred from Classic Engine to Identity Engine. There is no automated migration for mobile devices. Mobile devices will use Okta Verify.

  1. Determine the types of devices that you have:
    1. In the Admin Console, go to Security > Device Trust.
    2. For iOS Device Trust or Android Device Trust, if Enable iOS Device Trust or Enable Android Device Trust is selected, then you have mobile devices.

      3. For example:

  2. Complete one of the following, depending on the types of devices that you have:
    • Mobile and desktop (Windows or macOS) devices: proceed to Task 2: In your Classic Engine org, prepare mobile devices for the migration.
    • Mobile devices only: Start with Task 2: In your Classic Engine org, prepare mobile devices for the migration.
    • Desktop (Windows or macOS) devices only: Start with Task 3: Upgrade from Classic Engine to Identity Engine.

Task 2: In your Classic Engine org, prepare mobile devices for the migration

Mobile Device Trust is not eligible for migration to the Identity Engine, so it must be turned off before you upgrade. After the upgrade, you can still achieve Device Trust (Classic Engine) for mobile using Okta FastPass or Okta Verify.

  1. Change the app sign-on policy condition for mobile to Any:
    1. In the Admin Console, go to Security > Authentication Policies.

    2. Select the policy you want to update.

    3. Click the Rules tab.

    4. In the Sign On Policy section, locate any mobile device (iOS, Android, or Other mobile) policy rules that do not have the Device Trust (Classic Engine) state set to Any.
    5. Click Edit, and select Any.

    6. Click Save.
  2. Disable Mobile Device Trust policies:
    1. In the Admin Console, go to Security > General.
    2. In the Okta Mobile section, click Edit.
    3. In the APP SETTINGS section, clear the Apply device trust policies when accessing apps in Okta Mobile checkbox.

  3. Turn off Mobile Device Trust:

    When you turn off Mobile Device Trust, existing Mobile Device Trust will no longer work. To mitigate this, users will install Okta Verify in step 4.

    1. In the Admin Console, go to Security > Device Trust.
    2. If Enable iOS Device Trust is selected, click Edit.
    3. Clear the Enable iOS Device Trust checkbox.
    4. Click Save.
    5. If Enable Android Device Trust is selected, click Edit.
    6. Clear the Enable Android Device Trust checkbox.
    7. Click Save.

      8. For example:

  4. Deploy Okta Verify to mobile devices.

    See Okta Verify installation options for macOS, and Okta Verify installation options for Windows.

  5. Remove the Integrated Windows Authentication (IWA) routing rules:
    1. In the Classic Admin Console, go to Security > Identity Providers.
    2. Click IWA.

    3. Take a screenshot or make a note of your IWA routing rule. This information will be useful if a rollback is required later.
    4. A Failed to update OIE upgrade state error appears for the org Superuser. Click Dismiss.

    5. From the Active drop-down, click Deactivate.
    6. Click Delete.
    7. If required, repeat this procedure for any other IWA rules.

Task 3: Upgrade from Classic Engine to Identity Engine

At first, when you upgrade your Classic Engine to an Identity Engine, Okta FastPass is not enabled. Your existing Device Trust (Classic Engine) authentications (mutual TLS authentications) will continue to work on Identity Engine. This allows you to verify that Device Trust (Classic Engine) works on the Identity Engine, but your existing Device Trust (Classic Engine) capabilities are available if you need to revert back to Classic Engine.

During the upgrade any app sign-on policy Device Trust (Classic Engine) conditions are translated to Device Registered & Managed Conditions in the Okta Identity Engine. If the valid Device Trust (Classic Engine) certificate is present on the device, the server considers the device to be registered and managed. If a certificate does not exist on the device or if it exists but is not valid, the server considers the device to be unmanaged.

  1. Verify that you are using the Classic Engine.

    2. See Identify your Okta solution.

  2. Upgrade to the Identity Engine. Contact your Okta account team, to do this.

Task 4: Verify that Device Trust works on Okta Identity Engine

At this step, your org is now running on Identity Engine, Device Trust is enabled, and Okta FastPass is not enabled. The end user experience is the same as it was with the Classic Engine. If a user attempts to access an app that is protected by a device condition, Okta will challenge the browser to present the Device Trust certificate, and then validate it before the user is given access.

Do not remove Device Trust (Classic Engine) until Task 7: Remove Device Trust and decommission your IWA servers. Once you remove Device Trust (Classic Engine) from your org, there is no way to reverse this action.

  1. Verify that your Device Trust (Classic Engine) configuration migrated to the Identity Engine:
    1. In the Admin Console, go to Security > Device integrations.
    2. Click the Endpoint Management tab.
    3. Verify that you have a platform for each device type that you identified during Task 1: Identify the types of devices that you have within your organization: desktop (Windows or macOS), mobile, or desktop (Windows or macOS) and mobile.

      4. For example, if Windows Device Trust and macOS Device Trust was enabled in your Classic Engine, you will have a platform listed for each.

  2. Verify that your app sign-on policy includes a rule for registered and managed device conditions:
    1. In the Admin Console, go to Security > Authentication Policies.
    2. Select the policy you want to update.
    3. Click the Rules tab.
    4. View the Sign On Policy information. One of the rules in the policy should specify Device: Registered, Managed.

      5. For example:

  3. View the following System Log events, to verify that Device Trust is still working:
    • Authentication
      • DisplayMessage: Authentication of device through a certificate
      • EventType: user.authentication.authenticate
    • Enrollment
      • DisplayMessage: Device Trust certificate enrollment
      • EventType: user.credential.enroll
    • Issuance
      • DisplayMessage: Device Trust certificate issuance
      • EventType: pki.cert.issue
    • Revocation
      • DisplayMessage: Device Trust certificate revocation
      • EventType: pki.cert.revoke
    • Renewal
      • DisplayMessage: Device Trust certificate renewal
      • EventType: pki.cert.renew
  4. Verify the following items on multiple operating systems:
    • Verify that all existing use cases work. For example, users with Device-Trust-enabled desktop devices should be able to successfully authenticate.
    • Verify that all app sign-on policies migrated successfully. For apps that are protected by Device Trust, make sure a rule includes a condition for Managed and Registered.
    • Use existing mutual TLS (MTLS) certification.
    • Optional. Verify that certificate renewals work.
    • Optional. Verify that new enrollments work.

Task 5: Enable Okta FastPass for some users and verify that it works as expected

During this step of the migration, Okta initiates the Okta FastPass flow by probing for Okta Verify. Depending on whether an end user has Okta Verify installed on their device, one of the following occurs:

  • Okta Verify is not installed: Okta probes the end user’s device for the Okta Verify app and determines that the app is not installed, so the user is presented with the org sign in screen. After the user enters their username, Okta completes an MTLS challenge and the management attestation is gathered from the presence of the Device Trust certificate on the device.
  • Okta Verify is installed, inline enrollment is disabled, but an account has not been added: Inline enrollment for Okta Verify is disabled, so the user is presented with the org sign in screen. After the user enters their username, Okta completes an MTLS challenge and the management attestation is gathered from the presence of the Device Trust certificate on the device.
  • Okta Verify is installed, inline enrollment is enabled, but an account has not been added: When a user tries to access a Device Trust-protected app, Okta probes the end user’s device for Okta Verify. Since inline enrollment is enabled, the user is prompted to add their account to Okta Verify. The authentication flow completes without mutual TLS authentication because the Device Trust status was provided by Okta Verify.

See Okta Verify for Windows and Okta Verify for macOS for Windows and macOS end user documentation, and Device registration for information about what end-user experience to finish registration or enrollment.

  1. If you disabled mobile Device Trust and are ready to redeploy and set up mobile Device Trust, complete the Typical workflow for configuring Device Trust for mobile devices.
  2. Prepare enterprise devices for Okta FastPass. Push the latest version of Okta Verify app to all desktop devices. Using your MDM, turn on inline enrollment for some users.

    3. Users are automatically prompted to enroll in Okta Verify the next time they access Okta (after you complete step 4 in this section). If a user has installed Okta Verify and has added an account to Okta Verify (enrolled), they will now use Okta FastPass to log in by default and establish Device Trust. If the user does not have an account in Okta Verify, they will use the Okta Classic Engine Device Trust certificates to log in.

    See Okta Verify installation options for macOS, and Okta Verify installation options for Windows for information about the EnrollmentOptions Flag.

    At this point, Okta Verify is in a “ready” state. When you enable Okta FastPass (in step 4), users with Okta Verify are prompted to register in Okta Verify. This step allows you to rollout Okta FastPass and Okta Verify registration in a controlled manner to help with change management.

  3. Make sure a certificate authority (CA) is configured, and that management certificates are deployed to all desktop devices. You can use Okta as a CA, or provide your own CA.

    4. See Configure a Certificate Authority.

    For example, if you provide your own CA:

    1. In the Admin Console, go to Security > Authentication Policies.
    2. Click the Certificate Authority tab.

    3. Deploy management certificates to the devices.
  4. Update your App sign-on policy. Verify that you have a managed rule for each applicable platform, and change the User must authenticate with value to Any 1 factor type:
    • Allow Trusted macOS:
      • Platform: macOS
      • Device: Registered, Managed
      • User must authenticate with: Any 1 factor type
    • Allow Trusted Windows:
      • Platform: Windows
      • Device: Registered, Managed
      • User must authenticate with: Any 1 factor type

        • For example:

      See Configure an app sign-on policy for Okta FastPass for details about setting up your app sign-on policy.

  5. Enable Okta FastPass. This is a global setting, but only:
    • Users with inline enrollment (set up in step 2), or
    • Users that registered in Okta Verify and have a management certificate (set up in step 3) will have access to Okta FastPass.

    See Enable Okta FastPass.

    6. When you Enable Okta FastPass, make sure you select the Okta FastPass (all platforms) checkbox. This must be selected before you deactivate Okta Device Trust.

  6. Verify these user scenarios when Okta FastPass is enabled. If a user is:
    • Not enrolled in Okta Verify, but is enrolled with Device Trust, they should be able to successfully access apps that are managed.
    • Not enrolled in Okta Verify, they should be able to enroll in Okta Verify.
    • Enrolled in Okta Verify from a managed device, they should be able to successfully access apps that are managed.
    • Enrolled in Okta Verify from an unmanaged device, they should be able to access apps the same way they could before the migration.

Task 6: Enable Okta FastPass for all users

During this step, encourage users to enroll in Okta Verify and have management certificates on their devices. Ideally, you want all users to have an account on Okta Verify and be using Okta FastPass, so device probing succeeds and mutual TLS authentication (Device Trust [Classic Engine]) is not necessary.

  1. Request all users to enroll in Okta Verify (for example, push Okta Verify to all users).

    2. For clarity, two steps are required to set up Okta FastPass:

    1. Enable Okta FastPass (see Task 5: Enable Okta FastPass for some users and verify that it works as expected, step 4).
    2. Make sure end users enroll in Okta Verify. As an admin, you can push Okta Verify to devices as a managed app, and communicate with end users that they need to enroll with Okta Verify. In Task 5: Enable Okta FastPass for some users and verify that it works as expected, step 1, you did this for some users, but now you want all users to enroll with Okta Verify.

    For support, you may want to share this information with your end users:

    • Okta Verify for Windows for Windows end users
    • Okta Verify for macOS for macOS end users.
    • Okta Verify for iOS for iOS end users.
    • Okta Verify for Android for Android end users.

  2. Verify the following must-have items:
    1. Verify that Okta Verify was deployed to all users using your MDM (see Task 6: Enable Okta FastPass for all users, step 1).
    2. Verify that all users have a management certificate, using your MDM (see Task 5: Enable Okta FastPass for some users and verify that it works as expected, step 2).
    3. Verify that all user access is from devices with Okta Verify, and not Device Trust (Classic Engine) (see Task 4: Verify that Device Trust works on Okta Identity Engine, step 3).

Task 7: Remove Device Trust and decommission your IWA servers

When all of your users are using Okta FastPass, you can remove Device Trust.

It is recommended that you do not remove Device Trust (Classic Engine) until all your users are enrolled in Okta FastPass.

  1. View the System Log events, to make sure Device Trust signals no longer exist. (see Task 4: Verify that Device Trust works on Okta Identity Engine, step 3).

    2. If Device Trust signals exist, migrate these users to Okta Verify. Any users not using Okta Verify will be impacted.

  2. Deactivate Device Trust:
    1. In the Okta Admin Console, go to Security > Device Integrations.
    2. Click Endpoint Management.
    3. For the required Platform, click Actions > Deactivate.

      4. If you receive an error when you try to delete the platform, see "Deactivate this Device Trust (Classic Engine) configuration" error in Troubleshoot the migration of Device Trust to Okta FastPass.

      or

    4. Click Deactivate.
  3. Delete Device Trust:
    1. For the Inactive Platform, click Actions > Delete.
    2. Click Delete.
  4. Decommission the IWA servers from your infrastructure, and then remove the Device Registration task from your devices using your preferred tool.
