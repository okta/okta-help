Update Your Private App for iOS
  1. From the Okta Dashboard, click the Applications drop-down menu.
  2. From the Applications page, navigate to your private app.
  3. Click the Mobile tab.
  4. Click the Edit button
  5. From the Where is the app located drop-down menu, choose Update app to new version.
  6. Click Browse to find your .ipa file. Keep the following in mind before uploading.

Your app must:

  • Have a matching bundle ID.
  • Have a valid provisioning profile.
  • Have a higher version number than the existing version of the app.
  • Use version numbers with integers only. (Example: Ver 2.1.1). Use of any alphanumeric characters results in an error.

Once uploaded, your end users can update the app from the Mobile App Store in Okta Mobile.

End-User Experience for Updated Apps on iOS

When an end user goes to the Okta Mobile App Store, a section titled Update displays all apps with available updates.

  1. View the Update section.
  2. Tap the Update button of the app.
  3. After the updated app installs, your app icon moves from the Update section to the Installed Applications page. Screenshot

App Security for iOS

Apps installed through the Okta Mobile App Store are automatically secured through OMM. The apps remain secured as long as the device is enrolled in OMM.

To secure an app installed outside of the Okta Mobile App Store:

  1. Search for your app within the Mobile App Store.
  2. Select Secure.

If you uninstall the app, then re-install it through Okta Mobile Store, the app remains secured. You will only see the option to secure the app again if you remove OMM enrollment, then subsequently re-enroll. To learn more go to Enabling Mobile Access to Applications.

Installed apps that are not secured by OMM are displayed in a dedicated section of the page.

About Run Time Permissions for Android for Work (AfW)

You can specify how permissions (access by the app to storage, phone, etc) are granted: by admin or end user.

  • Permissions must be specified before deploying the app.
  • If permissions for an app change, the app becomes inactive until the admin specifies new permissions.
  • If an org enabled an Android and/or Samsung SAFE app, and then later enabled AfW, all active Android apps become inactive until the admin approves their permissions.
Set up Android Enterprise

Before you begin, make sure that you have:

  • An Android enterprise account. For details see: Managed Google Play Help.
  • Uploaded your Android app in Google Play for Work. For details, see Publish private apps.
  1. Enroll your Android enterprise account with Okta by navigating from the admin Dashboard, to Devices >Mobile Policies.
  2. Select Set up Android for Work > Create Managed Google Play Accounts enterprise.

  3. You will be re-directed to the Google Play Console where you must enroll your Android enterprise account. Use the same gmail account that you used to upload your private app, and that you will use to administer your Google Play store.
  4. After adding your email, you will be enrolled, and redirected to the Okta Mobile Policies page. Now you can follow the steps to Upload Your Native App.
