About Okta Mobile

Okta Mobile delivers Okta's Single Sign-On (SSO) experience to iPads, iPhones, and Android devices. When end users launch , they get immediate one-click access to all of their applications. From end users can enroll into Okta Mobility Management (OMM) if they choose, and if OMM is configured for your org.

You don’t have to purchase OMM to use . See Configure Okta Mobility Management.

Options that you configure in the interact with mobile device user settings and the state of the app. This interaction determines when users are challenged for multifactor authentication (MFA), and prompted to enter a PIN, fingerprint, or Face ID, to unlock .

Remarks and known limitations

  • Not all Security Assertion Markup Language (SAML) apps are accessible from mobile devices. SAML federation allows end users one-click access to supported apps. After authenticating with Okta, end users can access SAML apps without signing in to the app itself. Many SAML apps also provide a fallback method so end users can access the app by entering their app sign on credentials.

    While many apps, such as Salesforce and Box, support SAML federation with their mobile applications, not all do. Some Independent Software Vendors (ISVs) still require users to enter their app credentials in order to access the mobile version of the SAML app. To verify how a given app behaves, consult the ISV of the app.

  • If Okta Mobility Management (OMM) is not enabled for your org, apps in open directly in the browser when end users long-press an app.

  • is not supported for use with Identity Provider Routing Rules.

  • Downloading files from for Android webview is not supported. This restriction minimizes the threat from malware and prevents end users from preserving copies of company resources. enforces no restriction on opening files that don't require saving to disk.
  • End users can rate for iOS. The app prompts users to provide a rating. After they click Submit, users are redirected to the App Store page for to provide additional feedback. To dismiss this option, users can click Not now.
  • Active Directory-managed users can use PIN or FaceID to access even if their accounts are in Password Reset state. To temporarily deactivate user accounts, use the Suspend procedure. See Suspend or unsuspend users. If you set an AD-managed account into Password Reset status, the user can still access by PIN or FaceID authentication.
