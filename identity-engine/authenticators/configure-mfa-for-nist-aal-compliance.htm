Configure MFA for NIST AAL compliance

The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) Authenticator Assurance Levels (AAL) framework prescribes appropriate levels of authentication required to access a system or application.

The AAL framework defines authenticator assurance as the level of confidence an app or service has that the credential presented for access is truly in the possession of the person whose identity is being asserted to the app or service.

Okta enables you to configure your multifactor authentication (MFA) policies and rules to conform with the NIST AAL framework. This helps protect your resources against unauthorized access and potential data breaches.

The Authenticator Assurance Levels framework

There are three assurance levels in the framework. Each level represents a different strength level of the authentication process and the binding between an authenticator and the user's identity. The levels address the sensitivity and risk level of the protected data or system. They also define the characteristics of the authentication methods that must be used to achieve a specific level of authentication.

  • NIST AAL1 - Single-Factor Authentication: This level requires a single authentication factor to verify the user's identity, such as a knowledge factor, like a password, or a possession factor, like a smart card.
  • NIST AAL2 - Two-Factor Authentication: This level requires two authentication factors to verify the user's identity, such as a knowledge factor, like a password, and an inherence factor, like a biometric factor.
  • NIST AAL3 - Multifactor Authentication: This level requires two or more authentication factors to verify the user's identity, such as a knowledge factor, like a password, a possession factor, like a smart card, and an inherence factor, like a biometric factor.

    AAL3 provides a higher degree of security by requiring the following conditions:

    • Proof of possession of a cryptographic key.
    • Use of a hardware-based authenticator.
    • Use of an authenticator that provides phishing resistance.
    • Proof of possession and control of two distinct authentication factors.
    • Use of NIST-approved cryptographic techniques.

NIST authenticator types

NIST defines four authenticator types based on the characteristics of the authenticator and how it's used to verify the user's identity.

  • Memorized Secret: This authenticator type is based on something that the user knows, such as a password, PIN, or passphrase. The user must enter the correct information to prove their identity.
  • Possession: This authenticator type is based on something that the user possesses, such as a smart card, USB token, or mobile phone. The user must physically present the authenticator to prove their identity.
  • Inherence: This authenticator type is based on a characteristic of the user, such as a biometric characteristic like a fingerprint, iris scan, or facial recognition scan. The user's biometric characteristic is compared to a stored reference to verify their identity.
  • Location: This authenticator type is based on the user's physical location as determined by a GPS or other location-tracking technology. The user's location is compared to a predefined range to verify their identity.

  8. Enter a name and description for the new policy.
  10. In the Catch-all Rule, click Actions Edit .
  11. Change the AND User must authenticate option:
    • For AAL1 compliance, select Any 1 factor type / IdP.
    • For AAL2 compliance, select Any 2 factor types.
    • For AAL3 compliance, select Any 2 factor types.
  12. Clear all checkboxes for the AND Possession factor constraints are option. If you're seeking AAL3 compliance, leave the Phishing Resistance and Exclude Email and Phone authenticators check boxes selected.
  13. In the AND Re-authentication frequency is option, select Re-authenticate after. In the field and dropdown that appear, set the re-authentication frequency:
    • For AAL1 compliance, set the frequency to 30 days.
    • For AAL2 compliance, set the frequency to 12 hours.
    • For AAL3 compliance, set the frequency to 12 hours.
  14. If you're seeking AAL2 or AAL3 compliance, use mobile device management (MDM) software to lock devices at the operating system level and require the user to authenticate to unlock the device. Configure these lock times for locking devices:
    • AAL1: 12 hours.
    • AAL2: 30 minutes.
    • AAL3: 15 minutes.
