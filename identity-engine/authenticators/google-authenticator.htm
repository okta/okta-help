Add a policy

  1. Click Add Multifactor Policy.
  2. Enter a name.
  3. Assign to groups.
  4. Set to Optional or Required.
  5. Click Create Policy.
  6. To add one or more rules to the policy, see Configure an authentication enrollment policy rule.

Edit a policy

  1. Select the policy that you want to edit, and then click Edit.
  2. In Effective factors, set to Optional or Required.
  3. Click Update Policy.
  4. To add one or more rules to the policy, see Configure an authentication enrollment policy rule.

End-user experience

  1. Go to the Apple App Store or the Google Play Store and install on your device.
  2. In the web browser on your computer: When signing in to or accessing an -protected resource, enter your credentials and then click Next.
  3. On the Setup security authenticators page, click Set up.
  4. Select your device type, and then click Next.
  5. Perform the QR code scanning steps that apply to you:

    If your device can scan QR codes:

    1. Don’t click Next in the browser yet; instead, on your mobile device, launch .
    2. In , tap the + sign.
    3. Tap Scan a QR code and then point your camera at the QR code displayed in the browser on your computer. Your device camera scans the QR code automatically.
    4. In the web browser on your computer, click Next.
    5. In the Enter Code field, enter the setup key shown in on your mobile device.
    6. Click Verify.

    If your device can’t scan QR codes:

    1. Don’t click Next in the browser yet.
    2. In the web browser on your computer, click Can’t scan.
    3. In the field above the Next button, make a note of the string of numbers and letters.
    4. On your mobile device, launch .
    5. Tap the + sign.
    6. Tap Enter a setup key.
    7. In the Account field, enter your username.
    8. In the Key field, enter the string of numbers and letters that you made a note of earlier.
    9. Tap Add. The message Secret saved appears.
    10. In the web browser on your computer, click Next.
    11. In the Enter Code field, enter the setup key shown in on your mobile device.
    12. Click Verify.

About rate limiting for

To protect your sensitive corporate resources from unauthorized access, enforces a rate limit of five unsuccessful authentication attempts from over a rolling five-minute period. If unsuccessful authentications from exceed the rate limit:

  • Authentication from isn't allowed until the rate limit passes.
  • returns HTTP status code 429, indicating "too many requests".
  • A message appears on the user interface and is written to the System Log.

Important considerations

  • The time on the end user's device might not be the same as the time on the clock in the app. The app allows a time difference on the end-user device of up to two minutes earlier or later than the time in the app.
