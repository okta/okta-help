Configure policies to use as an authenticator

  1. Create an authentication enrollment policy for the authenticator. Select an Eligible authenticators option in the policy for the authenticator.

  2. Configure an authentication enrollment policy rule to define what your users access with this authenticator. Select an option in the User is accessing section of the rule.

  3. Create an [%=okta-feature-names.app sign-on policy%] for each app that you want to protect with smart cards.
  4. Add an [%=okta-feature-names.app sign-on policy%] rule. Select the options that apply to your configuration in the Possession factor constraints section.

End-user experience

There are multiple ways users can enroll their as an authenticator:

  1. During the sign-in process, they click the Sign-in with PIV button and follow the instructions to enroll their .
  2. During the step-up authentication, they identify themselves in the and get prompted to enroll their .
  3. They enroll their through End-User DashboardSettings.

Enroll multiple s

Users can have multiple active Smart Cards at a time. They can enroll different s for different IdPs associated with the authenticator.

If they lose their smart card, they must remove it from their account and enroll a new one.

Use for verification

You can require smart cards when users sign in or access a protected app. They must perform the verification within the time period you've configured. If they don't, the operation times out and they must authenticate again.

Sign in with or

Currently, if you configured both the Sign in with button and as an authenticator, users only see the option when they sign in. By enabling this feature, you can make both options available for users during the sign-in process.
