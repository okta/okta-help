Branding

Branding is a no-code tool for customizing the Okta interface.

Branding creates a seamless experience for your users. The page where users sign in displays your org's background and logo. Their End-User Dashboard is branded with your colors. If users encounter an error, that page maintains the theme. Even the email messages for account and password maintenance reflect your org's brand.

About multibrand customization

If your organization includes multiple subsidiaries or multinational components, allows to you to use distinct branding for each part. This feature provides an alternative to setting up multiple orgs, which can require higher maintenance, cause slower performance, and make errors more likely.

Concepts

Branding your org starts with a theme. Your org's theme consists of the following components:

  • Primary color: a 3-digit or 6-digit hex code
  • Secondary color: a 3-digit or 6-digit hex code
  • Logo: a non-square org logo
  • Favicon: a square, digital-friendly icon
  • Background: a PNG or JPG image for use as a sign-in page background

You can apply your theme to these pieces of the Okta interface:

Okta-hosted sign-in page When end users attempt to sign in to your applications, they are redirected to an Okta-hosted sign-in page. Your background image is used here, and your org logo appears above the sign-in fields. Your favicon appears in the browser tab for this page.
Okta-hosted error pages If an error occurs while they're signing in, Okta displays a default error page. Your secondary color is used for the background, and your org logo appears above the error message. Your favicon appears in the browser tab for this page.
End-User Dashboard After they've signed in, end users can securely access their applications on their Dashboard. Your primary color is used for this navigation menu, and your favicon appears in the browser tab for this page.
Okta communication Okta sends email notifications to end users when their passwords are reset or their accounts are activated or unlocked. Your primary color and org logo are used on email templates.

Customizations that you make using the code editors for the sign-in page and error pages will overwrite branding for those pages. For reset instructions, see Before you begin.
