Behavior after a rollback

Your org isn't restored to its previous state after you roll back to . If you made org changes in , some of those changes may persist when you return to . Other features are restored to the default settings.

Changes that persist

  • Users, groups, and applications that you created in persist when you roll back.

  • New users who signed up in persist when you roll back.

  • Recovery and authentication factors that users configured in persist when you roll back.

  • Password and profile changes that users made in persist when you roll back to .

  • If you enrolled Phone as an authenticator in , both SMS and Phone are available for authentication when you roll back to .

  • If you disabled Mobile Device Trust or Self-Service Registration in before your upgrade, these features remain disabled when you roll back. If you enable them again in , the settings are restored to the defaults.

  • Push notifications and TOTP are enabled, even if you didn't have them enabled in before the upgrade.

Changes that don't persist

  • New authentication policies, global session policies, and policy settings created in are restored to Okta default settings after rollback.

  • Changes to password recovery or MFA enrollments made in are restored to Okta default settings after rollback.

  • New functionality like is deactivated after rollback.

  • The Sign-In Widget is restored to the version after rollback.

  • Links in activation and recovery emails may not work after rollback.

  • Security Questions that were previously configured for both account recovery and MFA are reset to recovery only after rollback.

  • Users sessions are ended. They need to re-authenticate after you roll back.
