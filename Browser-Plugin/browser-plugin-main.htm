The lets you use single sign-on (SSO) for applications that require user credentials but don't support Security Assertion Markup Language (SAML). You can configure it for the users in your org and set it up for your own admin account.

How it works

The supports trusted and verified apps that are authenticated through Secure Web Authentication (SWA). When you launch an app integration from the , a browser tab opens to the app's URL. The plugin uses an encrypted SSL connection to obtain authentication information from and then passes that information to the app. The browser doesn't store credentials after authentication is complete.

If you haven't installed the but you have app integrations on your that require it, an installation link appears on your dashboard.

The doesn't work with apps that use federated authentication standards like SAML or OpenID Connect (OIDC).

Benefits

In addition to enabling secure SSO for your org, the makes password management easier.

  • Automatically sign in to apps: When you go to the sign-in page of an -enabled SWA app, the automatically inserts your credentials.

  • Automatically initiate an sign-in event: If you aren't signed in to and go directly to an -enabled app, a pop-up banner appears with a Sign in button. If you didn't enable automatic app sign-in, the banner can auto-fill your credentials.

  • Automatically insert passwords on password-update pages: If you go to an Okta-enabled SWA app where you're required to update your password, the banner can automatically insert your current password.

  • Generate a strong password: If you need to create or change a password for your SWA-based app, the can auto-generate a strong password and save it to your account.

  • Update your password: When you change your password in an -enabled SWA app, the offers the option to update with your new password.

  • Prevent web browsers from saving sign-in credentials: You can prevent web browsers from saving sign-in credentials for and for third-party apps that you access through the

  • Switch between multiple accounts: You're prompted to trust or reject subsequent accounts the first time you access them. Over time, you can access a list of accounts through the icon.
