Switch between accounts with

Use the Account Chooser in the to easily switch between multiple accounts. When you access an account through the for the first time, you are prompted to trust or reject the browser. Over time, you can build a list of accounts through the plugin to easily access your apps.

Before you begin

Ensure that is installed. If you can't find the icon in the toolbar, it may be hidden. To display unhide the plugin, complete these steps:

  • Chrome: Click the Chrome menu ( ) and navigate to More Tools Extensions to verify that the is enabled. Once you have confirmed that the plugin is enabled, click the jigsaw puzzle icon in the upper right portion of your browser and select the pin icon next to the .
  • Firefox: Click the Firefox menu ( ), click Customize, and drag the blue icon to the desired location on the browser toolbar.
  • Internet Explorer: From the Tools menu ( ), go to Manage add-ons Okta toolbar , and click Enable.
  • Safari: Right-click anywhere on the browser toolbar, click Customize Toolbar, and then drag the Your Apps icon to the desired location on the browser toolbar.

Start this procedure

  1. Trust an Okta account and add it to the account chooser
  2. Show the account chooser and switch accounts

Trust an account and add it to the account chooser

  1. While already signed in to , sign in to another account that you have not trusted When an account is trusted, the Okta browser plugin will sign the end user in to the account and start showing the account's applications in the Your Apps list. or accessed yet on this browser..

    2. On the , a banner prompts you to specify whether you trust the account. Some apps on the dashboard are accessible only after you trust the account.

  2. Click the blue icon in the browser toolbar.
  3. If the Trust / Reject options appear in the Your Apps menu, choose what applies in your case:
    • If you click Trust, the red banner disappears, the remainder of the apps on your dashboard become accessible, and the account is added to your account chooser list.
    • Click Reject if you don't trust the account or if you don't want the plugin to take any action on the account. If you decide later that you trust the account, you can display make the Trust option display by refreshing the page, or signing in to the account again.

      • Note: For Chrome, Edge, and Firefox browsers, a red square on top of the blue icon indicates that a Trust or Reject action is needed.
