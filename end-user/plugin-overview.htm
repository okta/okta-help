With the you automatically initiate an Okta sign-in to a broad range of applications that would otherwise require you to manually enter your credentials.

When you open an app from your , a new browser tab opens pointing to the app URL. The plugin uses an encrypted connection to obtain authentication information and other required data from , and then applies that information to the page. The plugin doesn't store your credentials after authentication.

To enhance security, the plugin only works with trusted and verified sites. If you haven't yet installed the but have one or more applications on your end-user dashboard that require it, you are prompted on the dashboard to install the plugin.

The plugin is updated frequently. You are prompted to install the latest version if necessary. See Okta Browser Plugin version history.

Install the plugin

To install the Okta Browser Plugin, refer to our installation guide.

What you can do with the

  • Automatically sign in to apps
  • Automatically initiate an [%=okta-variables.ProductName%] sign in
  • Automatically fill in credentials on sign-in pages
  • Automatically insert passwords on password-update pages
  • Update passwords
  • Switch between multiple [%=okta-variables.ProductName%] accounts
  • Prevent web browsers from saving sign-in credentials

Automatically sign in to apps

If you go directly to the sign-in page of certain apps in , the plugin automatically enters your credentials and signs you in without further interaction.

Automatically initiate an sign in

If you are not signed in to and go directly to an application that is -enabled, a pop-up banner appears with a sign-in button. When you attempt to sign in, an window launches and you can sign into the app without having to open the .

Automatically fill in credentials on sign-in pages

If you go directly to the sign-in page of some -enabled apps, the pop-up banner provides an option to auto-fill your credentials if automatic sign-in is not enabled.

Automatically insert passwords on password-update pages

When you are on a password update page of some -enabled apps, the pop-up banner can automatically insert your current password.

Update passwords

When you change your password in some -enabled apps, the plugin offers you the option to update with your new password.

Switch between multiple accounts

After you sign in to , you are prompted to trust or reject subsequent accounts the first time you access them. Over time, you can build a list of Okta accounts through the plugin to easily access your apps.

See Switch between accounts with Okta Browser Plugin.

You can prevent web browsers such as Chrome from saving your sign-in credentials for as well as for third-party apps that you access through the .
