Enable or update on macOS devices

If your device supports user verification, you can use it to verify your identity when you sign in with . When is required, you must enable to access your account.

If is enabled and you changed your settings, you receive a message:

Before you begin

Make sure you’re using a local device. You can't disable Touch ID in a remote session.

Start this task

  1. Open .

    2. For example, click the desktop shortcut, or if the app is running, from the menu bar extras, click the icon > Open.

  2. On the Accounts page, click the account you want to enable for.

    3. If an account has an indicator beside it, is required but not enabled, or it needs an update.

  3. On the Touch ID with Okta Verify page, click Next.

  4. Click Enable .

    5. If this is the first time you set up Touch ID, you must first set up your fingerprint in the system settings. Click Add fingerprint and follow the instructions. Place a finger on the Touch ID sensor when prompted. Ensure that the edges of your fingerprint are captured during Touch ID enrollment. Otherwise, you might receive an error when you enable Touch ID in .

    If prompted, click Yes, I've added a fingerprint.

  5. In the new tab that opened, enter your username.

  6. Click Next.
  7. Enter your password.

  8. Click Verify. A message confirms that your identity was verified.

    9. The enabled message confirms that your was successfully added to your account.
