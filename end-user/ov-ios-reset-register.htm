Add your Okta Verify account on a new iOS device from the Dashboard settings page

If you already use and want to set up your account on a new device, it might not be enough to just install the app on the new device. First, you might have to remove your existing enrollment either from your device, or from the Settings page of your organization’s apps dashboard. Then, you can add the account on the new device. Some restrictions apply.

This topic is for users who already have an account on a device. If you’re a new user who wants to set up for the first time, see Set up [%=okta-feature-names.Okta Verify%] on iOS devices.

Before you begin

Start this procedure

  • Task 1. In a browser, get a new enrollment QR code for [%=okta-feature-names.Okta Verify%].
  • Task 2. Install [%=okta-feature-names.Okta Verify%] and add an account.

Task 1. In a browser, get a new enrollment QR code for .

Task 2. Install and add an account.

  1. Go to your device, download the app from the Apple App Store and install it.
  2. Open and follow the instructions.
  3. Tap Add Account.
  4. Choose the type of account to add. Tap Organization.
  5. Choose your account setup method.
    • Ensure the code is shown on another device such as a laptop or desktop. Tap Yes, Ready To Scan, and point your camera at the QR code displayed in the browser on another device.
    • If you don’t know how to obtain the QR code, tap Where do I get my QR code?
    • If you can’t scan the QR code, tap No, Sign In Instead. Follow the instructions in Set up [%=okta-feature-names.Okta Verify%] on your iOS device by signing in to your account.
  6. If prompted, allow push notifications on your device so that you can approve future sign-in notifications without opening the app.
  7. If prompted, enable Face ID or Touch ID. Tap Enable.
  8. Tap Done to complete the account enrollment.
  9. If the following screen appears on your browser, click Finish. (This screen appears only in some cases).

is now set up on your device and your account is available in the app.
