Configure Face ID or Touch ID in your account on iOS devices

In some organizations, you can set up Face ID or Touch ID in as an additional way to confirm your identity when you sign in to apps protected by .

Tasks

  • Enable Face ID or Touch ID
  • Disable Face ID or Touch ID

Enable Face ID or Touch ID

If you didn’t enable Face ID or Touch ID during the account setup, you might be prompted to do so later.

If you change the Face ID or Touch ID settings on your device, you go out of sync with the settings. If prompted, sync or enable Face ID or Touch ID.

  1. Open and tap your account.
  2. On the Account Details page, go to the Options section and turn on Face ID or Touch ID.

  3. If prompted, go to your device Settings to configure Face ID or Touch ID. Then, return to to set up this type of verification in the app.
  4. If prompted, confirm your identity. Tap Sign in.
  5. Enter your organization's email address. This is the email where you received the setup instructions from your technology department.

    The Setup Complete screen indicates what authentication methods were enabled.

  6. Tap Done. The Face ID or Touch ID toggle is now on.

Disable Face ID or Touch ID

If Face ID or Touch ID is mandatory in your organization, you can’t disable it.

  1. Open and tap your account.
  2. On the Account Details page, go to the Options section and turn off Face ID or Touch ID.

  3. When prompted, confirm your identity by scanning your fingerprint or face.

Face ID or Touch ID is now disabled.
