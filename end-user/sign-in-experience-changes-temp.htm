Sign-in experience

If your organization now supports Identity Engine, your sign-in experience looks a little different:

  • There’s no security image.
  • There's no Remember me checkbox.

Some of the sign-in tasks are different, too. The following resources explain what's changed and link to more comprehensive documentation.

Keep me signed in

The Remember me checkbox has been changed to Keep me signed in. Depending on the policies your admin has set, your username may be pre-populated the next time you sign in from the same browser.

If the Keep me signed in checkbox appears on your sign-in page, select it to minimize username and security method prompts the next time you sign in. Depending on the policies your admin has set, you may not be required to re-authenticate at all or you may be able to skip verification of extra security methods.

Sign-in flow changes

Depending on the policies that your admin has configured, your sign-in flow may have changed.

If you see the Username and Password fields on the same page, enter your email address or username, your password, and then click Next. You may be prompted to verify with an additional security method on the next page.

If you only see a Username field, your org has enabled different security methods to help you sign in. Enter your email address with the full domain, and then click Next.

On the Security Methods page, select the option you want to use and complete the verification step.

If your org has advised you to set up FastPass, Sign in with FastPass appears on the same page as the Username field.

To use this option, you must have installed and set up on your device. If you use this option, clicking on the FastPass button allows you to skip the username entry and authenticate through FastPass if you’ve enabled it on your device or desktop. Depending on the policies, you may not be prompted for biometric authentication.

If you don’t have set up on your device, you can enter your username and click Next.

Select a security method

In , you may have been asked to select an “authentication factor” if your org had multifactor authentication enabled. Now, in , “authentication factor” is called “security method”.

also changes how you select the security method to use. Instead of using a dropdown list, you now see all available security methods on the panel, and you click Select for the one you want to use.
